## Pairing Rationale

**Dragon / Living Go** — In Go, a large connected group winding across the board is already called a dragon. Kajiwara's philosophy of living stones — each one breathing, each one pointing in the direction of the whole — is the dragon's body made into a game.

**Mantis Shrimp / AI Disciple** — The mantis shrimp sees in 16 color channels; humans see 3. It perceives reality through a sensory architecture that other animals cannot access. The AI disciple plays Go having perceived the board through dimensions of calculation that human intuition literally cannot follow. The 3-3 invasion on move 1 isn't aggression — it's a creature seeing in 16 colors telling you the corner was always red.

**Crane / Classicist** — Crane-style kung fu is one of the oldest schools — built on precision, deflection, and the belief that correct form *is* the technique. The crane moves through space with total economy. In China, Japan, and Korea — the three civilizations that gave the world Go — the crane means the wisdom of age carried lightly.

**Spider / Trickster** — The spider does not hunt. It architects. Every thread placed with intention, the full structure invisible until complete. Eight eyes see in all directions simultaneously. The web is the game.

**Eagle / Moyo Artist** — The eagle soars at altitude above local skirmishes and sees the board as one unified shape. Its presence casts a vast shadow. Prey doesn't feel the eagle's territory as walls — they feel it as something atmospheric, inescapable, already surrounding them before they looked up.

**Lion / Territorial Builder** — The lion does not hide. It owns the land openly, and everything on the savanna knows it. No deception, no trap — simply unambiguous claim repeated until the whole board is his. Wins by 2.5 points without drama.

**Praying Mantis / Blood Warrior** — Origin of Tángláng Quán — one of the oldest and most ferocious schools of Chinese kung fu, developed by watching a mantis fight a cicada and refuse to yield. Perfectly still, then strikes faster than the eye can follow. Strikes first. Does not wait to be threatened.

**Jaguar / Endgame Assassin** — The jaguar waits motionless for hours. Then one movement — a bite directly to the skull. Not a wound. A conclusion. The endgame assassin seems absent for 120 moves, then becomes a different entity. You realize you've lost after you've already lost.

**Crow / Ko Monster** — The only animal other than humans known to hold grudges, use tools, and play. Finds value in what others discard. Creates chaos with intention. Always — always — has one more threat.
